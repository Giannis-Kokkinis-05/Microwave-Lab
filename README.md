During our first lab I was tasked to create a low pass equal ripple filter with a cutoff frequency of 1.5GHz and 0.2dB ripple using transmission lines (MLIN equivalent to inductors, MLEF equivalent to capacitors). Because I used non ideal elements the cutoff frequency actually fell onto 1.7GHz. On the other hand, I actually found a fix to the cutoff frequency centering by changing the length of the first MLEF to mils as shown. The response dispalyed is the transmission coefficient.

During our second lab I had to impedence match a two port transistor using open/short (TLSC/TLOC) circuited stubs for each port. Firstly, I had to really precisely find all the electric angles on the smith chart by hand using rulers and a compass, and then insert them onto the program. On the contrary, since the smith chart has limited acurracy (the angle system's values change by 2 degrees every time) I had to vary the values at the decimal level a bit and found really exciting results! Specifically, the reflection coefficient of the first port ended up being at -55dB and for the second port -68dB and the two impedances were really close to 50Ohms! The two (open-circuited) stub matching circuit didn't work for the distances specified. The most engineering way to do was altering their positions, but my supervisor insisted on not including it in the report.
