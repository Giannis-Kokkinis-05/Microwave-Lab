During our first lab I was tasked to create a low pass equal ripple low pass filter with a cutoff frequency of 1.5GHz and 0.2dB ripple using transmission lines (MLIN equivalent to inductors, MLEF equivalent to capacitors). Because I used non ideal elements the cutoff frequency actually fell onto 1.7GHz. On the other hand I actually found a fix to the cutoff frequency centering by changing the length of the first MLEF to mils as shown. The response dispalyed is the transmission coefficient.

During our second lab I had to impedence match a two port transistor using open/short (TLSC/TLOC) circuited stubs for each port. Firstly, I had to really precisely find all the electric angles on the smith chart by hand using rulers and a compass, and then insert them onto the program. On the contrary, since the smith chart has limited acurracy (the angle system's values change by 2 degrees every time) I had to vary the values at the decimal level a bit and found really exciting results! Specifically, the reflection coefficient of the first port ended up being at -55dB and for the second port -68dB and the two impedances really close to 50Ohms! The two stubs matching circuit didn't work for the distances specified and had changed the positions but my supervisor insisted on not including it in the report.
